DOOM Eternal (PC, 2020)

One of the most exciting singleplayer first-person shooters in many years, DOOM Eternal brings a fresh brand of exciting, fast-paced FPS gameplay with an amazingly fun and difficult mix of gauntlent style enemy encounters, parkour, and resource management, accompanied with beautiful visuals and a rocking soundtrack.

Super Metroid (SNES, 1994)

Super Metroid captures an unmatched feeling of ominous atmosphere and rewarding exploration that no game before its release had ever whipped up, and many of the games it inspired struggle to match. Helped birth one of the best genres in gaming, Metroidvanias, and still holds up amazingly to this day.

Pokémon Platinum (DS, 2008)

My personal candidate for the best Pokémon title, Platinum boasts one of the most challenging campaigns in the series with a diverse array of Pokémon to choose from, and hours worth of content spread across a diverse region. (And also some personal nostalgia)
